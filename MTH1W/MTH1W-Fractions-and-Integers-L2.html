Number Sense and Algebra

Lesson 2: Operations with Fractions & Integers

Core Concepts: Integer Rules

Integers are whole numbers, including zero and negative numbers. The key is to remember the rules for adding, subtracting, multiplying, and dividing.

Adding & Subtracting Integers

  • Same Signs: Add the numbers and keep the sign. Ex: $(-3) + (-4) = -7$
  • Different Signs: Subtract the smaller number from the larger number and keep the sign of the larger number. Ex: $(-5) + (+9) = +4$
  • Subtracting a negative is the same as adding a positive. Ex: $(+6) - (-2) = 6 + 2 = 8$

Multiplying & Dividing Integers

  • Same Signs: The result is always **positive**. Ex: $(-5) \times (-4) = +20$
  • Different Signs: The result is always **negative**. Ex: $(+10) \div (-2) = -5$

Core Concepts: Fraction Rules

Working with fractions requires finding common denominators for addition and subtraction, and simpler rules for multiplication and division.

Adding & Subtracting Fractions

You must have a **common denominator** before you can add or subtract.

$$ \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{3} \rightarrow \frac{3}{6} + \frac{2}{6} = \frac{5}{6} $$

Multiplying Fractions

Multiply the numerators together, and multiply the denominators together. Simplify if possible.

$$ \frac{2}{3} \times \frac{4}{5} = \frac{2 \times 4}{3 \times 5} = \frac{8}{15} $$

Dividing Fractions

To divide, you **flip the second fraction** (find its reciprocal) and then multiply.

$$ \frac{1}{2} \div \frac{3}{4} \rightarrow \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{4}{3} = \frac{4}{6} = \frac{2}{3} $$

Independent Practice

Solve the following problems.

1. Calculate: $(-15) - (-8)$

$(-15) + 8 = -7$

2. Calculate: $\frac{3}{4} \times \frac{2}{5}$

$\frac{3 \times 2}{4 \times 5} = \frac{6}{20} = \frac{3}{10}$

3. Calculate: $\frac{4}{5} - \frac{1}{3}$

$\frac{12}{15} - \frac{5}{15} = \frac{7}{15}$
