Lesson for September 8, 2025: Slope and Y-Intercept

How can a simple straight line describe everything from your cell phone bill to the distance a car travels?

Core Concepts: The Anatomy of a Line

Every straight line can be described by a simple equation. This equation has two critical parts that tell us the line's story: its steepness and its starting point.

1. The Slope ($m$)

The slope tells us how steep a line is. It's the "rate of change." Think of it as instructions for moving from one point on the line to another.

We calculate it as the "rise" (vertical change) divided by the "run" (horizontal change).

$$m = \frac{\text{rise}}{\text{run}} = \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}$$
  • A positive slope means the line goes up from left to right.
  • A negative slope means the line goes down from left to right.

2. The Y-Intercept ($b$)

The y-intercept is the point where the line crosses the vertical y-axis. It's the "starting value" or "initial amount" when $x=0$.

The Magic Formula: $y = mx + b$

This is the slope-intercept form of a linear equation. If you know the slope ($m$) and the y-intercept ($b$), you know everything about the line.

Guided Practice: A Real-World Scenario

Scenario: Mobile Phone Plan

A phone company offers a plan that costs a $20 flat fee each month, plus $10 for every gigabyte (GB) of data used. Let's model this relationship.

Step 1: Identify the "Starting Value" (Y-Intercept, $b$)

Step 2: Identify the "Rate of Change" (Slope, $m$)

Step 3: Write the Equation in $y = mx + b$ form

Solution Walkthrough

$\text{Starting Value (flat fee)} \implies b = 20$
$\text{Rate of Change (cost per GB)} \implies m = 10$
$\text{Equation is } C = 10d + 20$

Independent Tasks

Apply your knowledge to the following scenarios. Use the stylus zones to show your work.

Task 1: The Coffee Shop

The graph below shows the cost of buying coffee at a local shop, which includes a one-time fee for a reusable mug.

Your Workspace:

Y-intercept ($b$): The line starts at $y=5$. So, the mug costs \$5.
Slope ($m$): From (0, 5), the line goes to (2, 10). Rise = $10-5=5$. Run = $2-0=2$. Slope $m = 5/2 = 2.5$. Each coffee costs \$2.50.
Equation: $C = 2.50n + 5$, where $C$ is cost and $n$ is the number of coffees.

Task 2: The Taxi Fare

A taxi service charges a \$3.00 pickup fee, plus \$1.50 for every kilometre driven.

Your Workspace:

Y-intercept ($b$): The pickup fee is the starting cost, so $b = 3.00$.
Slope ($m$): The rate per kilometre is the slope, so $m = 1.50$.
Equation: $F = 1.50k + 3.00$

Recap & Exit Card Quiz

Key Takeaways:

  • The equation of a line can be written as $y = mx + b$.
  • $m$ is the slope: It measures the steepness or the rate of change.
  • $b$ is the y-intercept: It is the starting value.
