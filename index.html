Lesson for August 28, 2025: Slope and Y-Intercept

How can a simple straight line describe everything from your cell phone bill to the distance a car travels?

Core Concepts: The Anatomy of a Line

Every straight line can be described by a simple equation. This equation has two critical parts that tell us the line's story: its steepness and its starting point.

1. The Slope ($m$)

The slope tells us how steep a line is. It's the "rate of change."

$$m = \frac{\text{rise}}{\text{run}}$$

2. The Y-Intercept ($b$)

The y-intercept is the point where the line crosses the vertical y-axis. It's the "starting value" when $x=0$.

The Magic Formula: $y = mx + b$

This is the slope-intercept form of a linear equation.

Guided Practice: A Real-World Scenario

Scenario: Mobile Phone Plan

A phone company offers a plan that costs a $20 flat fee each month, plus $10 for every gigabyte (GB) of data used. Let's model this relationship.

Step 1: Identify the "Starting Value" (Y-Intercept, $b$)

Step 2: Identify the "Rate of Change" (Slope, $m$)

Step 3: Write the Equation in $y = mx + b$ form

Solution Walkthrough

$\text{Starting Value (flat fee)} \implies b = 20$
$\text{Rate of Change (cost per GB)} \implies m = 10$
$\text{Equation is } C = 10d + 20$
